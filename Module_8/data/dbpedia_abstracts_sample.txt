[1] Port of Rotterdam
The Port of Rotterdam is the largest port in Europe and is located in Rotterdam, Netherlands. It is managed by the Port of Rotterdam Authority and serves as a major gateway for logistics and maritime trade in Europe.

[2] Maersk
Maersk is a Danish shipping conglomerate headquartered in Copenhagen, Denmark. It operates global container shipping and logistics services and frequently connects with major international ports.

[3] Panama Canal
The Panama Canal is a large artificial canal in Panama that connects the Atlantic Ocean and the Pacific Ocean. It is a critical infrastructure corridor for global maritime transportation.

[4] Suez Canal
The Suez Canal is an artificial sea-level waterway in Egypt connecting the Mediterranean Sea and the Red Sea. It is one of the most important routes for international shipping between Europe and Asia.

[5] Port of Busan
The Port of Busan is a major sea port in South Korea. It handles large container throughput and operates as a key hub for Northeast Asian maritime trade.

[6] Cross-document context
Maersk vessels and other global carriers use strategic chokepoints such as the Panama Canal and Suez Canal to optimize intercontinental shipping routes.

[7] Network context
Major ports such as Rotterdam and Busan are integrated with inland logistics systems, supporting multimodal freight flows between seaborne and land transportation networks.
